About Me

My dissertation focuses on elementary teachers' experiences during a one year cycle of an adaptive professional development program.

At IU, I was a research assistant on a project titled Representations for Teachers as Learners (RepTaL) . This a multi-year, cross-site project where I serve as a coach and data analyst.

Most of my work involves elementary science teaching. I have experience teaching both informal and formal science education at the K-12 and college levels.I am currently a 5th grade science teacher and science department chair at Kairos Academies, a charter school in Saint Louis, MO.

My responsibilities at IU included being an associate instructor as well as managing/coordinating the Saturday Science informal science learning program. I was the instructor for the elementary science methods, advanced elementary science methods, elementary math and science field experience, and an introductory course to scientific inquiry. I am interested in the best ways to develop material and engage students in the classroom through phenomenon-based learning.
